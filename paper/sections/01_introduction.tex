\section{Introduction}

Forecast accuracy is a necessary but incomplete objective for operational demand planning. Real planning systems execute planning signals, such as inventory targets, replenishment quantities, capacity allocations, staffing levels, or production schedules. A forecast that improves numerical accuracy can still create operational burden if it causes frequent model switching, large target jumps, or infrastructure updates that planners and downstream systems cannot absorb.

This paper studies the planning-infrastructure gap: the forecasting layer can adapt faster than the execution system. The goal is to evaluate demand planning policies as multi-objective systems that balance forecast accuracy, inventory cost, planning stability, model switching cost, and execution adaptability.
